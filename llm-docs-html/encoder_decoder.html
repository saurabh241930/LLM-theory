The Sequence-to-Sequence translation pipeline

The Mathematics of Context Compression

If you want to translate a sentence from English to French, you cannot translate word-for-word. "The white house" becomes "La maison blanche" (The house white). Grammar rules and sequence lengths change entirely.

The solution is the Encoder-Decoder. First, the Encoder reads the entire English sentence and mathematically crushes it into a single, dense geometry point called the Context Vector. Then, the Decoder uses only that compressed context to generate the French sentence entirely from scratch.

The Context Bottleneck: Notice how the pure mathematical essence of "The white house" is literally squeezed into a single circle (vector). The Decoder is blind to the English words; it only sees the numerical "fingerprint" inside the Context Vector.

While this architecture revolutionized translation in 2014, it struggled with massive paragraphs because squishing 500 words into a single Context Vector inevitably caused meaning to spill over and be lost. This exact limitation is what directly forced the invention of the Attention Mechanism in 2017 to bypass the bottleneck!

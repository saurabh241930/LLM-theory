1. Summary

Random Forest is an ensemble of many Decision Trees. It uses Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregating) to create different subsets of data for each tree and Feature Randomness to ensure trees are decorrelated.

4. Why it Works

While a single tree may overfit to noise, a "forest" of trees cancels out individual errors. This is known as reducing Variance without significantly increasing Bias.

5. Real-World Case Study: Customer Churn Prediction

The Problem: A Telecom giant (like Verizon) wants to predict which subscribers will cancel their plan next month.

The Solution: Random Forest analyzes hundreds of disparate signals:

  • Contract type (Month-to-month vs 2-year)
  • Total monthly charges
  • Number of "Technical Support" calls made

Why Random Forest? Individual decision trees might over-focus on one signal (e.g., just contract price). Random Forest Aggregates many trees to find the stable "signal" in the "noise" of complex customer behavior.

1. Project Description

Customer churn refers to when a customer stops doing business with a company. It is much cheaper to retain an existing customer than to acquire a new one. In this project, we predict churn for a Telecommunications provider.

Goal: Identify high-risk customers and understand the key drivers (features) behind their exit.

3. Data Preprocessing

We convert categorical variables into numerical ones using One-Hot Encoding and handle missing values in 'TotalCharges'.

In [3]:
df = pd.read_csv('churn_data.csv')
df['TotalCharges'] = pd.to_numeric(df['TotalCharges'], errors='coerce')
df.fillna(df['TotalCharges'].mean(), inplace=True)

# Drop CustomerID as it has no predictive power
df.drop('customerID', axis=1, inplace=True)

# One-Hot Encoding
df = pd.get_dummies(df, drop_first=True)

5. Training the XGBoost Model

We choose XGBoost for its speed and performance on tabular data. We also focus on 'Feature Importance' to provide business insights.

In [5]:
X = df.drop('Churn_Yes', axis=1)
y = df['Churn_Yes']

X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(X, y, test_size=0.2, random_state=101)

model = xgb.XGBClassifier(n_estimators=200, learning_rate=0.05)
model.fit(X_train, y_train)

# Feature Importance
importance = model.feature_importances_
feature_imp = pd.Series(importance, index=X.columns).sort_values(ascending=False)

6. Conclusion

Business Insight: The model revealed that Tenure (loyalty duration) and Contract Type are the strongest predictors. Customers on Month-to-Month contracts are significantly more likely to churn than those on 1-year or 2-year contracts. The company should offer incentives to switch to long-term contracts.

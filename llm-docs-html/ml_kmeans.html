1. Summary

k-Means is the most popular unsupervised learning algorithm. It groups data points into k clusters based on their proximity to a "centroid" (the center of a cluster). It is an iterative algorithm that keeps shifting centroids until they converge.

2. Visual Flow

4. The Elbow Method

How do we choose the best "k"? We plot the WCSS (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares) for different values of k. The point where the rate of decrease shifts significantly (the "elbow") is usually the optimal number of clusters.

5. Scenarios & Use Cases

📂 Use Cases

  • Customer Segmentation (Marketing).
  • Image Compression (Color quantization).
  • Document Categorization.

⚠️ Caveats

  • Sensitive to initialization (Random Centroid choice).
  • Assumes spherical clusters - fails on complex shapes.
  • Sensitive to outliers (they can pull centroids away).

6. Real-World Case Study: Marketing Customer Segmentation

The Problem: A brand has 1 million customers but doesn't know how to tailor its email campaigns.

The Solution: k-Means groups customers into 3 clusters:

  • Cluster 1: High Spenders (Luxury focus)
  • Cluster 2: Frequent small buyers (Budget/Deal focus)
  • Cluster 3: Lapsed customers (Re-engagement focus)

Why k-Means? When you have Unlabeled Data (you don't know who the "Luxury" buyers are beforehand), k-Means discovers these hidden structures automatically using distance-based grouping.

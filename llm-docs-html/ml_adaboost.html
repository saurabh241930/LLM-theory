1. Summary

AdaBoost (Adaptive Boosting) works by training "Weak Learners" (Decision Stumps) sequentially. After each round, it increases the weights of misclassified points, forcing the next stump to focus on the "harder" cases.

4. Real-World Case Study: Face Detection (Viola-Jones)

The Problem: Early digital cameras needed to find faces in a photo in real-time using very weak mobile processors.

The Solution: AdaBoost combined thousands of "Weak Learners" (simple light/dark filters called Haar Features).

  • Stump 1: Is the eye region darker than the nose? (Yes/No)
  • Stump 2: Is the mouth region darker than the chin? (Yes/No)

Why AdaBoost? Sequential Focus. AdaBoost allows the system to quickly discard "non-face" areas (like blank walls) using just a few stumps, and then spend more "mental energy" (weighted stumps) on complex areas like the eyes and nose to confirm a face exists.

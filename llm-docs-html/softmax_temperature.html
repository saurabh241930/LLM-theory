The Mathematics of Exponentials

We say Temperature "squishes" or "flattens" the logits, but what does that look like geometrically?

The raw logits from the neural network are fixed locations on the X-axis. The Softmax function is an exponential curve. By dividing the X value by Temperature (T) before taking the exponential, you physically change how steep the curve is.

At T < 1, the curve becomes infinitely steep (Greedy). At T > 1, the curve flattens completely.

Watch the Curve: The Yellow Dots are fixed logits (x=2, x=3.5, x=4). Their horizontal positions never shift, because the neural network output hasn't changed.

However, as you increase the Temperature, the Red Exponential Curve physically flattens out to y=1. The mathematical result is that the "tallest" dot loses its absolute exponential advantage over the lower dots, bringing their Y values infinitely closer together.

When you normalize these closer Y values (divide by the sum to get 100%), they become a flat, creative, and random probability distribution.

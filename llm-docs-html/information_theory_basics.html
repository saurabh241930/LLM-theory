Information Theory in LLMs

Entropy, Cross-Entropy, and KL Divergence

The Mathematics of Surprise

Every Large Language Model is ultimately a probability engine. To train them, we rely entirely on the foundations of Information Theory, pioneered by Claude Shannon in 1948.

1. Entropy (H)

Entropy is the mathematical measure of unpredictability or "surprise" in a probability distribution.

H(P) = - Σ P(x) * log2(P(x))

If a coin is perfectly weighted to land on Heads 100% of the time, the Entropy is 0. There is no surprise. If a coin is perfectly fair (50/50), the Entropy is exactly 1 bit. In LLMs, a high entropy output distribution means the model is deeply confused about what word comes next.

2. Cross-Entropy Loss

This is the literal Loss Function used to train every single LLM on Earth including GPT-4.

Let P be the True Distribution (the actual word that appears next in the training data, e.g., 100% probability on "Apple").
Let Q be the Estimated Distribution (what the LLM generated, e.g., 40% "Apple", 60% "Banana").

CrossEntropy(P, Q) = - Σ P(x) * log(Q(x))

Cross-Entropy measures the total cost of predicting Q when the truth is P. Because P is essentially "1" for the correct word and "0" for everything else, this algorithm ruthlessly penalizes the model for assigning a low probability to the correct word.

3. KL Divergence (Kullback-Leibler)

How different are two distributions? KL Divergence calculates exactly how much extra information (or penalty) you pay by using distribution Q to approximate distribution P.

DKL(P || Q) = CrossEntropy(P, Q) - Entropy(P)

In Deep Learning, we usually cannot measure KL Divergence directly because we don't know the absolute true entropy of human language. But because Entropy(P) is a fixed constant (the dataset doesn't change), minimizing Cross-Entropy Loss is mathematically identical to minimizing KL Divergence. This is why we use Cross-Entropy to train models.

Note: KL Divergence is heavily used in Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO / RLHF) to prevent the fine-tuned model from drifting too far away from the original base model.

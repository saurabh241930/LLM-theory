The Evolution of NLP: The Path to Transformers

How machines went from understanding words to understanding context
1980s-2010s: RNNs (Recurrent Neural Networks)

Designed for sequences. They process words one by one, passing a "hidden state" (memory) forward.

Failure: They suffer from Vanishing Gradients. By the time they reach the 20th word, they've forgotten the 1st word.

1997: LSTMs (Long Short-Term Memory)

Introduced a "Cell State" and gates (forget gate, input gate) to selectively keep important information for much longer.

Failure: Sequential processing—they cannot be parallelized, making them too slow for massive datasets.

2014: Seq2Seq & Encoder-Decoders

The architecture that powered Google Translate. An Encoder reads the input sentence into a "Context Vector," and a Decoder generates the translation.

Failure: The "Bottleneck Problem"—compressing a 100-word sentence into one vector loses detail.

2017: The Transformer ("Attention Is All You Need")

The breakthrough. It removed recurrence entirely and replaced it with Self-Attention.

graph LR A[Input Sequence] --> B[Positional Encoding] B --> C[Multi-Head Attention] C --> D[Feed Forward Network] D --> E[Output Layers]

Result: Massively parallelizable and captures Global Context instantly. This enabled GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer).

2018-Present: The Era of Pre-trained LLMs

The birth of BERT (Encoder-only) and GPT-1/2/3/4 (Decoder-only), leading to the current Generative AI revolution.

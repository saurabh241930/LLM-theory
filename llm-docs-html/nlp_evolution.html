The Evolution of NLP: The Path to Transformers

How machines went from understanding words to understanding context
1980s-2010s: RNNs (Recurrent Neural Networks)

Designed for sequences. They process words one by one, passing a "hidden state" (memory) forward.

Failure: They suffer from Vanishing Gradients. By the time they reach the 20th word, they've forgotten the 1st word.

1997: LSTMs (Long Short-Term Memory)

Introduced a "Cell State" and gates (forget gate, input gate) to selectively keep important information for much longer.

Failure: Sequential processing—they cannot be parallelized, making them too slow for massive datasets.

2014: Seq2Seq & Encoder-Decoders

The architecture that powered Google Translate. An Encoder reads the input sentence into a "Context Vector," and a Decoder generates the translation.

The "Encoder-Decoder Bridge" (Cross-Attention)

In modern Transformers, the Decoder doesn't just receive one vector. It uses Cross-Attention to "look back" at the Encoder's hidden states at every step of generation.

  • Query (Q): Comes from the Decoder's previous layer.
  • Keys (K) & Values (V): Come from the Encoder's final output.

Teacher Forcing: During training, we feed the correct previous token into the decoder instead of its own (potentially wrong) prediction to speed up convergence.

2017: The Transformer ("Attention Is All You Need")

The breakthrough. It removed recurrence entirely and replaced it with Self-Attention.

graph LR A[Input Sequence] --> B[Positional Encoding] B --> C[Multi-Head Attention] C --> D[Feed Forward Network] D --> E[Output Layers]

Result: Massively parallelizable and captures Global Context instantly. This enabled GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer).

2018: BERT (Encoder-only)

Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers. Unlike GPT, BERT looks at both the left and right context simultaneously.

BERT Training Objectives

  • Masked Language Modeling (MLM): Hiding 15% of tokens and asking the model to predict them. This forces deep contextual understanding.
  • Next Sentence Prediction (NSP): Predicting if Sentence B follows Sentence A.
  • [CLS] Token: A special token at the start used for classification tasks (e.g., sentiment analysis).
Limitation: BERT cannot generate text well because it "sees" the future during training. It is for Understanding, not Generation.

Model Architecture Comparison

Type Mechanism Examples Primary Use
Encoder-only Bidirectional self-attention. BERT, RoBERTa Classification, NER, Logic.
Decoder-only Causal (Masked) self-attention. GPT-3, GPT-4, Llama Text Generation, Chat.
Encoder-Decoder Cross-attention between both. T5, BART Translation, Summarization.

BERT: Bidirectional Encoder Representations

Why generation needs masks, but understanding needs the future

The Mathematics of Blindness

Generative models like GPT (Decoder-only) are mathematically forced to be blind to the future. During training, their Attention matrices are strictly masked so that Token #4 can only look at Tokens 1, 2, and 3. You cannot train an AI to predict the next word if you let it peek at the answer key.

But what if you aren't trying to generate text? What if you just want to classify if an email is Spam, or perform Named Entity Recognition? By mathematically unmasking the matrix and allowing Bidirectional Attention, BERT achieved state-of-the-art context understanding.

BERT (Bidirectional): The [MASK] token geometrically pulls attention vectors from both the left ("went to the") AND the right ("to deposit cash"). Because it has access to the word "deposit" in the future, it perfectly predicts that the missing word is "bank".

This is called Masked Language Modeling (MLM). We randomly hide 15% of the words and force the network to use global context to fill the holes.

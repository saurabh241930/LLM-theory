Alignment refers to the process of training a Large Language Model to behave in accordance with human values and specific operational constraints. While pre-training provides general-purpose linguistic knowledge, the resulting model (often called a "Base Model") is not inherently optimized for being helpful or conversational. Alignment techniques transform these base models into Assistant Models by refining their outputs to be Helpful, Harmless, and Honest (H-H-H).

Instruction Tuning (SFT)

The first stage of alignment is Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT). A base model is fed high-quality pairs of instruction and response (e.g., from the Alpaca or ShareGPT datasets). After this stage, the model understands the "format" of an assistant-user interaction but lacks a fine-grained sense of which nuance humans prefer.

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)

RLHF was the breakthrough that enabled models like GPT-4. It works in three distinct phases:

  1. Sampling: Generate multiple responses for the same prompt.
  2. Ranking: Humans rank the responses from best to worst.
  3. Reward Model: Train a separate neural network to predict these human scores.
  4. Policy Optimization: Use Reinforcement Learning (PPO) to shift the model's behavior toward high-reward outputs.

Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)

DPO is a more recent, computationally efficient alternative to RLHF. Instead of training an explicit reward model and using complex Reinforcement Learning, DPO treats preference learning as a simple classification problem. It bypasses the reward model entirely by optimizing the model's likelihood of choosing "Winning" responses over "Losing" responses directly.

Constitutional AI (CAI) & RLAIF

Constitutional AI (popularized by Anthropic) removes the "human-in-the-loop" bottleneck by using a second AI to supervise and align the primary AI based on a written list of rules—the Constitution.

The RLAIF Pipeline:

  1. Critique: The model generates an initial response. A "Critique LLM" evaluates it based on Constitutional principles (e.g., "Is this respectful?").
  2. Revision: The model rewrites its response to address the critique.
  3. Preference Learning: The "Feedback LLM" looks at multiple revisions and picks the best one based on the Constitution.
  4. Final Alignment: The model is fine-tuned (SFT or DPO) on these AI-generated preferences.

Key Benefit: Scaling alignment to massive datasets without needing thousands of human labelers.

When to Align

💡 Pro-Tip: Most developers should start with a model that has already been aligned (e.g., Llama-3-Instruct). You only need to perform your own alignment if you need to enforce highly specific corporate standards or brand-voice preferences that generic alignment doesn't cover.

Case Study: RSA Sales QA Dashboard

The RSA Sales QA Dashboard requires a bot that is perfectly aligned with "Insurance Ethics." during training, the developers used DPO (Direct Preference Optimization) to provide the model with pairs of responses. For example, given a transcript where an advisor slightly pressures a customer, the model was trained to prefer a response that flags the ethical violation over one that praises the successful sale. This alignment ensures the AI acts as a safeguard for the company's legal reputation rather than just a sales-tracking tool.

Key Takeaways

✅ Alignment ensures models are safe and useful for humans.
✅ SFT is the "Foundation" of alignment; DPO/RLHF is the "Polish."
✅ DPO is rapidly replacing RLHF for smaller production teams.
✅ Always aim for the H-H-H criteria in your evaluation rubrics.

Advanced Feature Engineering

Visualizing brutal transformations on raw data

1. The Log Transform (Taming Outliers)

The Problem: When training on Income or Housing Prices, the data is violently right-skewed. 99% of people make $50k, but Elon Musk makes $100 Billion. The neural network's MSE loss will hyper-focus solely on fixing Elon's error, ignoring everyone else.

The Geometrical Fix: The Natural Log ln(x) mathematically crushes exponentially large numbers while barely touching small numbers. Click the button to watch a massively skewed wealth distribution physically compress into a learnable Bell Curve.

2. Min-Max Scaling (Squashing Space)

The Problem: Imagine a dataset with Age (0-100) and Salary ($0 - $200,000). During gradient descent, the Salary weights will receive gradients 2000x larger than the Age weights. The landscape becomes an unnavigable ravine, and the optimizer crashes.

The Geometrical Fix: We force everything into the exact same physical bounding box. Click the button to watch wild, unbound data dimensions physically squash into a tiny, perfect 1x1 mathematical square.

3. Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

The Problem: You have 10,000 features, but half of them are highly correlated (e.g., "Square Footage" and "Number of Rooms"). Keeping both wastes RAM and induces overfitting (The Curse of Dimensionality).

The Geometrical Fix: PCA calculates the Eigenvectors of the data to find the single diagonal line that contains the highest variance. It rotates the entire universe so that line becomes the X-axis. Then, it ruthlessly deletes the Y-axis. Click the buttons to visually reduce 2D data down to a 1D line하면서 virtually zero information loss.
